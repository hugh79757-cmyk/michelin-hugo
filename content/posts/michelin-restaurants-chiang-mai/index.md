From street food stalls to charming restaurants, Chiang Mai showcases the essence of Thai cuisine, particularly Northern Thai specialties, making it an exciting destination for food enthusiasts.

The Bib Gourmand designation highlights restaurants that offer high-quality food at reasonable prices. In Chiang Mai, these establishments provide a fantastic opportunity to enjoy authentic flavors without breaking the bank. 

One standout is **CHAWEE**, a Bib Gourmand restaurant that takes pride in its intimate and personalized dining experience. Named after the chef-owner's grandmother, this establishment offers a warm atmosphere complemented by thoughtfully prepared Thai dishes. 

Another notable mention is **Magnolia Café**, which features a cozy setting with communal tables and bold colors. This Bib Gourmand spot is perfect for those looking to enjoy traditional Thai dishes in a friendly environment. 

For a taste of local street food, **Lung Khajohn Wat Ket** is a worth trying. Located opposite Wat Ket Karam, this humble eatery specializes in khao kriap pak mo, a steamed rice flour snack that has become a favorite among locals. Similarly, **Tue Ka Ko Na Prince** has been serving crispy deep-fried taro with a loyal following for decades, showcasing the charm of Chiang Mai's street food culture.

If you're in the mood for Northern Thai cuisine, **Gongkham** offers a relaxed vibe in a Lanna-style wooden house, while **Huen Muan Jai**, tucked away in a quiet alley, has built a reputation for its authentic dishes over the past decade. 

Noodle lovers should not miss **Khao Soi Mae Sai**, known for its rich and slightly spicy curry, or **Guay Jub Chang Moi Tat Mai**, which serves a unique peppery broth with Sai Ua sausage. Both establishments exemplify the culinary diversity found in Chiang Mai.

Other Bib Gourmand restaurants worth mentioning include **Rasik Local Kitchen**, which provides a thoughtful take on Thai cuisine, and **Saiyut and Doctor Sai Kitchen**, where pan-Thai dishes are served following traditional recipes. 

## Cuisine Styles You Will Find in Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai's culinary scene is diverse, with a particular emphasis on Northern Thai cuisine. This regional style is characterized by its use of fresh herbs, spices, and locally sourced ingredients. The city also embraces a variety of other styles, including traditional Thai, street food, and contemporary interpretations of classic dishes.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/michelin-restaurants-chiang-mai/body_3.jpg)
*Photo by [Kirandeep Singh Walia](https://www.pexels.com/@kirandeepsingh) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


Northern Thai cuisine is well-represented in the Michelin selections, with 14 establishments focusing on this style. Dishes such as khao soi, a coconut curry noodle soup, and sai ua, a spicy sausage, are staples that reflect the region's unique flavors.

In addition to Northern Thai, the city boasts 12 restaurants serving traditional Thai cuisine, ensuring that visitors can enjoy beloved classics. Street food options are plentiful, with six Bib Gourmand selections highlighting the casual dining experience that Chiang Mai is known for.

Contemporary dining is also on the rise, with several establishments offering creative twists on traditional dishes. Restaurants like **Blackitch** and **Belén by Paulo Airaudo** showcase a modern approach to cooking while still honoring local ingredients and flavors.

## How to Book and Tips for Dining

When planning your culinary journey in Chiang Mai, it's advisable to make reservations, especially for popular Bib Gourmand and selected restaurants. While some street food spots operate on a first-come, first-served basis, many sit-down restaurants can fill up quickly, particularly during peak dining hours.

It's also beneficial to explore local dining customs. For instance, many Thai restaurants encourage sharing dishes, allowing diners to sample a variety of flavors. Don't hesitate to ask for recommendations from the staff, as they can guide you to the best dishes based on your preferences.

Lastly, be prepared to enjoy a meal at a leisurely pace. Dining in Thailand is often an extended affair, offering a chance to savor each dish and enjoy the company of friends or family.

 Chiang Mai's Michelin-rated restaurants present an exciting opportunity to explore the depth of Thai cuisine, particularly its Northern Thai roots. With a variety of options that cater to different tastes and budgets, the city stands out as a culinary destination worth experiencing.
