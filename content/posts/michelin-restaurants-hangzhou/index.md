Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang province, is renowned for its stunning landscapes and deep history. This city is not only a visual delight but also a culinary destination that has garnered attention from food enthusiasts worldwide. The Michelin guide recognizes a total of 76 restaurants in Hangzhou, showcasing the diversity and richness of its culinary scene. With 12 one-star establishments, one two-star restaurant, and 26 Bib Gourmand selections, Hangzhou offers a wide range of dining experiences that cater to various tastes and budgets.

Nestled amidst lush vegetation, this restaurant presents a menu that features familiar Zhejiang dishes, including the celebrated Xihu fish. The serene setting enhances the dining experience, making it a remarkable spot for those seeking an elevated culinary adventure.

## One-Star Gems

The one-star restaurants in Hangzhou offer an impressive array of cuisines, each with its unique ambiance and specialties. Sense, known for its innovative approach, features full-length windows that provide a view of the lush greenery surrounding Xiang Lake, creating a perfect backdrop for a delightful meal.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/michelin-restaurants-hangzhou/body_3.jpg)
*Photo by [Acres of Film](https://www.pexels.com/@acres-of-film-698445032) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*

Jie Xiang Lou, located in a bamboo forest, exudes tranquility and serenity. This restaurant focuses on Jiangnan dishes, providing a taste of the region's culinary heritage. In contrast, Song captures the essence of Ningbo cuisine, with its jade-coloured archways and silk-clad panels that evoke a classical Hangzhou garden setting.

Wild Yeast presents a modern take on Chinese contemporary cuisine, where diners can enjoy views of the open kitchen in a softly lit environment. Hangzhou House, situated near the Lingyin Temple, offers rustic-style dining with a menu that reflects the rich flavors of Zhejiang cuisine.

L'éclat 19, a French contemporary restaurant, is nestled in a wooded valley by West Lake, providing a picturesque setting for those looking to indulge in French-inspired dishes. Yu Zhi Lan, a landmark of Sichuan cooking, recently opened its doors in Hangzhou, bringing the flavors of Chengdu to this lively city.

For those interested in innovative dishes, Ambré Ciel showcases chef Alan Yu's skills in an atmospheric setting. Xin Rong Ji, located in Xixi National Wetland Park, offers a unique dining experience outside the city center, while Jin Sha combines classical Chinese style with a lakeside view. Longjing Manor, tucked away in a quiet alley, surprises with its spacious interior and welcoming atmosphere.

Guiyu (Xihu) stands out with its elegant design amidst an osmanthus forest, where the sweet scent of the flowers enhances the dining experience. Each of these one-star establishments provides a unique perspective on Hangzhou's culinary landscape, inviting diners to explore the region's flavors and traditions.

## Bib Gourmand: Best Value Fine Dining

The Bib Gourmand selections in Hangzhou highlight restaurants that offer exceptional food at reasonable prices. Qing Tao is a notable example, where the owner, with nearly 30 years of experience, still takes an active role in the kitchen, ensuring authenticity in every dish.

Xin Liu He has been a favorite since 1987, drawing crowds for its traditional Hangzhou fare. Lai Cui Mian Guan (Ji Mao Road) is a tiny noodle joint that has gained a loyal following for its delicious offerings, despite its higher prices. Hui Xin Xiao Chi Dian is a busy dim sum shop that attracts locals during lunchtime with its tasty options.

Fu Yuan Ju (Shangcheng) prides itself on home-style dishes, often resulting in queues during mealtimes. Zhi Zhu, located at the foot of Lingyin Temple, offers a delightful vegetarian menu in a rustic garden setting. Meanwhile, Er Ba Jiu Su Mian Guan is a popular vegan spot near Fajing Temple, known for its packed atmosphere and flavorful dishes.

For those seeking traditional snacks, Wang Ri Shun Hao serves up small eats made in the authentic style of Jinhua. Nan Feng Fan Dian, a no-frills shop, may be easy to overlook, but it offers a satisfying dining experience. Yue Ji (Binjiang) is famous for its unique dishes that may sound unappealing but are celebrated for their flavors.

Rong Xian Mian Guan (Qianjiang Road) has a long history and is known for its signature sliced noodles, while Datou Yingshi Xiaoguan offers a modern Jiangnan style in a cultural park setting. These Bib Gourmand selections prove that exceptional dining experiences can be found without breaking the bank.

## Cuisine Styles You Will Find in Hangzhou

Hangzhou's culinary scene is predominantly influenced by Zhejiang cuisine, which is characterized by its fresh ingredients and delicate flavors. The city boasts 22 restaurants focusing on this regional cuisine, showcasing dishes that highlight the local produce and traditional cooking techniques. Hang Zhou cuisine, with 13 restaurants dedicated to it, reflects the city's rich culinary heritage, offering dishes that are both comforting and familiar.

Noodles are another significant part of Hangzhou's dining culture, with nine establishments specializing in this versatile dish. Vegetarian options are well represented, with six restaurants catering to those seeking plant-based meals. Cantonese, Ningbo, and Chao Zhou cuisines are also present, providing a broader range of flavors for diners to explore.

Innovative and contemporary approaches to Chinese cuisine are evident in three and two restaurants, respectively, showcasing chefs' creativity and modern techniques. The diverse culinary landscape in Hangzhou ensures that there is something for everyone, regardless of dietary preferences.

## How to Book and Tips for Dining

Reservations are highly recommended, especially for popular restaurants. Many establishments may require advance booking, particularly during peak dining times or weekends. It’s advisable to check the restaurant’s policy regarding reservations, as some may accept walk-ins while others may not.

When dining in Hangzhou, be open to exploring various cuisines and styles. Each restaurant has its own unique ambiance and specialties, so taking the time to experience different venues can enhance your culinary journey. Whether you are indulging in a luxurious meal at a two-star restaurant or enjoying a casual bite at a Bib Gourmand spot, Hangzhou's dining scene offers a rich mix of flavors and experiences waiting to be discovered.
