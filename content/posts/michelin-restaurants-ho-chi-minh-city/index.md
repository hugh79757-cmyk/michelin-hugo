Ho Chi Minh City, a lively metropolis in [Vietnam](https://daytrips.techpawz.com/posts/hanoi-day-trips/), is home to a diverse culinary landscape that has garnered international attention. With a total of 75 Michelin-rated restaurants, the city showcases a range of dining experiences, from street food stalls to innovative fine dining establishments. The Michelin Guide has recognized five one-star restaurants and 21 Bib Gourmand selections, highlighting the city's culinary prowess and the dedication of its chefs to elevate Vietnamese cuisine.

Among the one-star establishments, **CieL** stands out with its innovative approach to dining. Nestled in a modern villa in Thao Dien, this restaurant offers an intimate setting that complements its creative dishes. Another noteworthy mention is **Coco Dining**, which combines a chic atmosphere with a whisky bar and private party villa, making it a unique dining experience in the city.

**Akuna** embraces its name, which means "flowing water," by crafting dishes that reflect a harmonious blend of flavors. Located in a serene environment, it invites diners to appreciate the artistry of its menu. For those seeking Cantonese cuisine, **Long Trieu** provides a lavish dining experience within a luxury hotel, adorned with jade stonework that enhances the ambiance.

Lastly, **Ănăn Saigon** presents a contemporary take on traditional Vietnamese dishes, crafted by chef-owner Peter Cuong Franklin, who brings his international experience back to his hometown. Each of these one-star restaurants offers a distinct perspective on dining, showcasing the creativity and skill of their chefs.

## Bib Gourmand: Best Value Fine Dining

In addition to the one-star establishments, Ho Chi Minh City boasts an impressive selection of Bib Gourmand restaurants, which are celebrated for offering exceptional food at reasonable prices. **Bò Kho Gánh** is renowned for its beef stew, served in a stone bowl, allowing diners to savor the rich flavors that define this beloved dish. Similarly, **Phở Miến Gà Kỳ Đồng** captures the essence of Vietnamese street food with its outstanding chicken phở, a dish that has garnered a loyal following among locals.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/michelin-restaurants-ho-chi-minh-city/body_3.jpg)

For a taste of traditional Vietnamese fare, **Nhà Tú** provides a tranquil oasis adorned with green plants and nostalgic decor, inviting guests to unwind while enjoying their meals. Another popular choice is **Bánh Xèo 46A**, where diners can choose to sit al fresco by the open kitchen, experiencing the lively atmosphere while indulging in delicious bánh xèo.

**Mặn Mòi** has established itself as a landmark in the dining scene, known for its nature-inspired interior and commitment to quality. The humble **Phở Phượng** prepares each bowl of phở with meticulous care, ensuring that every ingredient shines through. For noodle enthusiasts, **Phở Hương Bình** and **Phở Lệ (District 5)** have been serving authentic phở for decades, maintaining their reputation for excellence.

Vegetarian options also abound, with **Chay Garden (District 3)** offering a serene dining experience in a quiet pedestrian street, while **Hum Garden** features a charming courtyard that enhances its plant-based offerings. Other notable mentions include **Cục Gạch Quán**, where diners can enjoy traditional Vietnamese dishes in a setting that combines nature with culinary art.

## Cuisine Styles You Will Find in Ho Chi Minh City

The culinary scene in Ho Chi Minh City is as diverse as its population. Vietnamese cuisine takes center stage, with 12 Michelin-rated restaurants dedicated to showcasing traditional flavors and innovative interpretations. Noodle dishes are particularly prominent, with 10 establishments specializing in various preparations, including phở, a beloved national dish.

Street food is an integral part of the city's dining culture, with nine Bib Gourmand selections reflecting the rich offerings available from street vendors and small eateries. Vietnamese contemporary cuisine is also well-represented, with seven restaurants pushing the boundaries of traditional recipes to create modern interpretations.

In addition to local flavors, European contemporary and French cuisines have made their mark, with five and four Michelin-rated restaurants, respectively. Cantonese and vegetarian options are also available, catering to a range of tastes and dietary preferences.

## Price Ranges and What to Expect

When dining in Ho Chi Minh City, one can expect a variety of price ranges that cater to different budgets. One-star restaurants typically fall within the ₫₫₫ to ₫₫₫₫ range, offering a more upscale dining experience with innovative dishes and exceptional service. Bib Gourmand establishments, on the other hand, provide excellent value, with prices often starting from as low as ₫, making them accessible to a broader audience.

For those seeking a casual dining experience, street food stalls and local eateries offer delicious options at even lower price points. This diversity in pricing allows diners to explore the city's culinary offerings without breaking the bank, whether they are indulging in a fine dining experience or enjoying a bowl of phở from a street vendor.

## How to Book and Tips for Dining

Reservations are recommended for one-star restaurants, especially during peak dining hours or weekends. Many establishments allow for online bookings, but it is wise to check their policies in advance. For Bib Gourmand and selected restaurants, walk-ins are often welcome, but arriving early can help secure a table, particularly at popular spots.

When dining in Ho Chi Minh City, it is advisable to explore local specialties and be open to trying new dishes. Engaging with the staff can enhance the experience, as they often have valuable insights into the menu and can recommend dishes that highlight the restaurant's strengths. Whether dining at a Michelin-starred restaurant or a local street food stall, the key is to savor the flavors and enjoy the lively culinary landscape that Ho Chi Minh City has to offer.
