## Michelin Dining in Singapore: An Overview

The Michelin Guide has become a benchmark for food enthusiasts, and [Singapore](https://visafree.techpawz.com/posts/singapore-visa-free/) is no exception. With a total of 293 Michelin-rated restaurants, the city boasts an impressive array of culinary options. Among these, 29 establishments have been awarded one star, 7 have received two stars, and 3 prestigious restaurants have earned the coveted three-star rating. Additionally, there are 89 Bib Gourmand selections that highlight exceptional value for money, making high-quality dining accessible to a broader audience.

## Cuisine Styles You Will Find in Singapore

Singapore's culinary landscape is a melting pot of flavors, influenced by its diverse cultural heritage. The top cuisines represented in the Michelin Guide include:

- **Street Food**: With 151 Michelin-rated street food options, Singapore is famous for its hawker centers and local delights.
- **Sushi**: Featuring 11 sushi restaurants, the city offers authentic Japanese dining experiences.
- **Cantonese**: An equal number of 11 restaurants serve Cantonese cuisine, showcasing traditional dishes with a modern twist.
- **Innovative**: With 10 innovative establishments, chefs in Singapore are pushing culinary boundaries and experimenting with flavors.
- **Singaporean**: Celebrating local flavors, 9 restaurants focus on authentic Singaporean dishes.
- **Italian**: Featuring 8 Italian restaurants, the city offers a taste of [Italy](https://tours.techpawz.com/posts/best-tours-rome/) with contemporary flair.
- **French**: With 7 French establishments, diners can indulge in classic and modern French cuisine.
- **European Contemporary**: Another 7 restaurants offer a contemporary take on European dishes.
- **French Contemporary**: 6 establishments serve modern French cuisine with innovative approaches.
- **Peranakan**: Representing the unique Straits Chinese culture, 6 restaurants focus on Peranakan cuisine.

## Price Ranges and What to Expect

When dining at Michelin-rated restaurants in Singapore, expect a range of price points to accommodate different budgets. The restaurants span from affordable street food options priced at around $ to high-end dining experiences that can cost $$$$.

- **$**: Street food and casual dining options, such as **Hill Street Tai Hwa Pork Noodle** and **Candlenut**.
- **$$**: Mid-range restaurants offering quality dishes without the high price tag, like **Summer Pavilion** and **Shisen Hanten**.
- **$$$**: Fine dining establishments, including **Iggy's** and **Jaan by Kirk Westaway**, where you can expect a more refined experience.
- **$$$$**: High-end dining experiences, such as **Les Amis** and **Odette**, where culinary artistry and luxury come together.

## How to Book and Tips for Dining

Reservations are highly recommended, especially for popular Michelin-starred restaurants. Many establishments offer online booking options, while others may require a phone call. It’s advisable to book well in advance to secure your table, particularly for the three-star and two-star restaurants.

When dining at Michelin-rated establishments, consider the following tips:

1. **Dress Code**: Many high-end restaurants have a dress code, so it’s best to check in advance.
2. **Tasting Menus**: Consider opting for a tasting menu to experience a range of dishes and the chef's signature creations.
3. **Pairing Options**: Inquire about wine or beverage pairings to enhance your dining experience.
4. **Timing**: Arrive on time for your reservation, as many restaurants have strict seating policies.

With its rich culinary landscape and diverse Michelin-rated options, Singapore is a haven for food lovers. Whether you’re indulging in street food or savoring a multi-course meal at a three-star restaurant, the city promises an unforgettable dining experience that reflects its vibrant culture and culinary innovation.
