Xiamen, a coastal city in Fujian Province, boasts a rich culinary landscape that has garnered significant recognition in the Michelin guide. With a total of 42 Michelin-rated restaurants, the city showcases a variety of dining experiences, from high-end establishments to budget-friendly options. The Michelin awards reflect the diverse food culture of Xiamen, with a strong emphasis on local Fujian cuisine, which is celebrated for its unique flavors and fresh ingredients. The presence of three Michelin-starred restaurants and 31 Bib Gourmand selections highlights the city’s commitment to quality dining.

Among the notable one-star establishments, Fleurs Et Festin stands out with its Chao Zhou cuisine, housed in a charming three-storey historic building. This restaurant offers a blend of tradition and modernity, creating a welcoming atmosphere that complements its culinary offerings. The price range for dining here is moderate, falling between ¥3,000 and ¥8,000.

Hokklo, another one-star restaurant, embraces the essence of Fujian hospitality, providing a luxurious dining experience in an elegantly furnished space. With prices ranging from ¥8,000 to ¥20,000, it is a place where guests can indulge in the rich flavors of Fujian cuisine.

Yanyu (Jiahe Road) is the third one-star restaurant, known for its modern styling and loyal customer base that has supported it for over a decade. The establishment also falls within the expensive category, with prices ranging from ¥8,000 to ¥20,000, making it a popular choice for those seeking quality dining in a contemporary setting.

## Bib Gourmand: Best Value Fine Dining

Xiamen's Bib Gourmand selections highlight the city’s ability to deliver exceptional dining experiences at more accessible price points. Zhen Zhen Hai Li Jian is a standout, where the owner takes pride in personally managing the restaurant while serving high-quality seafood dishes, all for under ¥3,000.

![Photo](https://pub-2f5c7af1c303419a933069212bc25874.r2.dev/etap/michelin-restaurants-xiamen/body_3.jpg)
*Photo by [jason hu](https://www.pexels.com/@hujason) on [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)*


For noodle lovers, Lu Niang Zi (Huli) offers a delightful menu specializing in beef noodle soup and noodles dressed in beef ragout, also priced under ¥3,000. Similarly, Yue Hua Sha Cha Mian has been a staple for over 40 years, known for its traditional noodle dishes that continue to attract a loyal clientele.

Hao Shi Lai is a family-run seafood business that has been part of the local culinary scene since 1993, offering a menu that resonates with many Xiamenians’ childhood memories, all within a moderate price range of ¥3,000 to ¥8,000.

Other notable mentions include Lao Song Bian Shi Dian, famous for its mini pork wontons, and Wu Lan Sha Cha Mian, both offering budget-friendly options for those looking to enjoy local flavors without breaking the bank.

Xiao Cheng Xi, located by the pier, provides a picturesque dining experience with views of Gulangyu, while Chic 1699, a flagship of a local chain, offers a modern take on Fujian cuisine, both priced moderately.

Vegetarian options are also available at Wuwei Natural Food, which features ocean views and a menu focused on fresh, natural ingredients, as well as Si Xia Li (Huli), where the chef-owner boasts nearly three decades of culinary experience.

## Cuisine Styles You Will Find in Xiamen

The culinary scene in Xiamen is predominantly influenced by Fujian cuisine, which is represented by 19 Michelin-rated establishments. This style of cooking emphasizes the use of fresh seafood, local ingredients, and intricate techniques. Noodles, a staple in the region, are featured prominently with eight Michelin-rated options, showcasing various preparations and flavors.

Seafood is another significant aspect of Xiamen's dining culture, with three Michelin-rated restaurants dedicated to this category. The coastal location ensures that the seafood served is fresh and flavorful, making it a highlight of many local menus.

Small eats and vegetarian options are also well-represented, allowing diners to explore a variety of tastes and textures. Chao Zhou and Sichuan cuisines add to the diversity, providing a broader culinary experience for those visiting the city.

## Price Ranges and What to Expect

Dining in Xiamen offers a range of price points to cater to different budgets. One-star restaurants tend to fall within the moderate to expensive categories, with prices typically ranging from ¥3,000 to ¥20,000. Bib Gourmand establishments, on the other hand, provide excellent value, with many options available for under ¥3,000.

Expect a focus on fresh ingredients and local flavors, especially in Fujian cuisine. The ambiance in higher-end restaurants is often elegant and thoughtfully designed, while budget-friendly spots may offer a more casual atmosphere without compromising on quality.

## How to Book and Tips for Dining

While many restaurants in Xiamen welcome walk-ins, it is advisable to make reservations, especially for one-star establishments like Hokklo and Yanyu (Jiahe Road), which can be quite popular. Reservations can typically be made by phone or through the restaurant's official channels.

When dining in Xiamen, consider trying a variety of dishes to fully appreciate the local flavors. Many restaurants offer shared plates or small portions, allowing for a more extensive tasting experience. Additionally, engaging with the staff about the menu can provide insights into the best dishes to try and any seasonal specialties available.

In summary, Xiamen's Michelin dining scene offers a rich mix of culinary experiences that reflect the city's unique cultural heritage. From high-end restaurants to budget-friendly eateries, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this coastal city.
