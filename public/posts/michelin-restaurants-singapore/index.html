Singapore, a vibrant and multicultural city-state, is not only known for its stunning skyline and bustling streets but also for its dynamic food scene. The Michelin Guide has recognized this gastronomic diversity, awarding a total of 293 restaurants across various categories. From street food stalls to high-end dining establishments, Singapore offers a plethora of culinary experiences that reflect its rich heritage and innovative spirit.

Michelin Dining in Singapore: An Overview
#

The Michelin Guide has become a benchmark for food enthusiasts, and Singapore is no exception. With a total of 293 Michelin-rated restaurants, the city boasts an impressive array of culinary options. Among these, 29 establishments have been awarded one star, 7 have received two stars, and 3 prestigious restaurants have earned the coveted three-star rating. Additionally, there are 89 Bib Gourmand selections that highlight exceptional value for money, making high-quality dining accessible to a broader audience.

Three-Star and Two-Star Excellence
#

At the pinnacle of the Michelin hierarchy are the three-star restaurants, which represent the epitome of culinary artistry and excellence. In Singapore, there are three outstanding establishments that have achieved this remarkable accolade:

Photo by Utpal Sarkar on Pexels

  • Les Amis | 3 Stars | French | $$$$
    Renowned for its haute cuisine, Les Amis offers a dining experience that emphasizes creative freedom and the chef’s vision.

  • Odette | 3 Stars | French Contemporary | $$$$
    Located within The National Gallery, Odette is a jewel of the Singapore dining scene, blending modern French cuisine with artistic presentation.

  • Zén | 3 Stars | European Contemporary | $$$$
    The brainchild of a celebrity chef, Zén is a culinary journey that showcases innovative European dishes in a beautifully restored shophouse.

For those seeking two-star experiences, Singapore features seven remarkable restaurants:

  • Cloudstreet | 2 Stars | Innovative | $$$$
    Inspired by literature, Cloudstreet offers a unique dining concept that pays homage to diverse culinary traditions.

  • Sushi Sakuta | 2 Stars | Sushi | $$$$
    This elegant sushi restaurant features a simple yet refined atmosphere, focusing on the artistry of sushi-making.

  • Thevar | 2 Stars | Innovative | $$$$
    Chef Mano Thevar’s creative menu combines Indian traditions with European influences, resulting in a memorable dining experience.

  • Jaan by Kirk Westaway | 2 Stars | British Contemporary | $$$
    This romantic restaurant captures the essence of fine dining with exceptional food and service.

  • Saint Pierre | 2 Stars | French Contemporary | $$$$
    Offering modern French cuisine with Asian influences, Saint Pierre boasts stunning views of Marina Bay.

  • Meta | 2 Stars | Innovative | $$$
    Chef Sun Kim’s culinary creations reflect a blend of Korean traditions and contemporary techniques.

  • Shoukouwa | 2 Stars | Sushi | $$$$
    With a focus on refined sushi, Shoukouwa is led by a Japanese head chef who champions elegance in every dish.

One-Star Gems
#

Singapore’s one-star restaurants showcase a diverse range of cuisines and styles, each offering a unique dining experience. Here are some notable mentions:

  • Omakase @ Stevens | 1 Star | Innovative | $$$$
    This intimate venue features a multi-course menu crafted by a chef from Osaka, highlighting the artistry of Japanese cuisine.

  • Lerouy | 1 Star | French Contemporary | $$$
    With a fresh new image, Lerouy invites diners to enjoy its innovative French dishes in a charming setting.

  • Hill Street Tai Hwa Pork Noodle | 1 Star | Street Food | $

  • Iggy’s | 1 Star | European Contemporary | $$$
    Known for its elegant ambiance, Iggy’s provides diners with a front-row view of the kitchen’s culinary magic.

  • Waku Ghin | 1 Star | Japanese Contemporary | $$$$

  • Ma Cuisine | 1 Star | French | $$$
    With its charming décor and authentic French fare, Ma Cuisine creates a cozy dining experience.

  • Nouri | 1 Star | Innovative | $$$$
    Nouri’s concept of “crossroads” cooking explores the intersections of cultures and ingredients, resulting in a captivating menu.

  • Summer Pavilion | 1 Star | Cantonese | $$

  • CUT | 1 Star | Steakhouse | $$$
    Celebrity chef Wolfgang Puck’s CUT is synonymous with high-quality steaks and bold flavors.

  • Shisen Hanten | 1 Star | Sichuan, Cantonese | $$
    This luxurious restaurant offers a refined dining experience with a focus on Sichuan and Cantonese cuisine.

  • Sushi Ichi | 1 Star | Sushi | $$$$

  • Burnt Ends | 1 Star | Barbecue | $$$
    This popular barbecue restaurant, led by Australian chef David Pynt, is celebrated for its flavorful dishes.

  • Labyrinth | 1 Star | Innovative, Singaporean | $$$
    Chef LG Han takes familiar local flavors and transforms them into innovative dishes that evoke nostalgia.

  • Imperial Treasure Fine Teochew Cuisine (Orchard) | 1 Star | Teochew | $$
    This restaurant specializes in traditional Teochew fare, emphasizing fresh seafood and authentic recipes.

  • Chaleur | 1 Star | Innovative | $$$
    Japanese chef Kawano’s 10-course menu showcases creativity and culinary excellence.

  • Araya | 1 Star | Innovative, Chilean | $$$$
    This establishment features ingenious creations by Chilean chefs Araya and Guerrero, reflecting their culinary journey.

  • Buona Terra | 1 Star | Italian Contemporary | $$$
    Located in a refurbished colonial house, Buona Terra offers an intimate dining experience with contemporary Italian dishes.

  • Pangium | 1 Star | Peranakan | $$$
    With a lush garden view, Pangium provides a delightful setting for enjoying Peranakan cuisine crafted by owner-chef Malcolm Lee.

  • Candlenut | 1 Star | Peranakan | $$
    Influenced by his mother’s cooking, Chef Malcolm Lee creates a serene dining atmosphere with airy interiors.

  • Jag | 1 Star | French Contemporary | $$$
    Since 2023, Jag has captivated diners with its innovative French dishes in a pristine, stylish setting.

Bib Gourmand: Best Value Fine Dining
#

For those seeking exceptional dining experiences without breaking the bank, Singapore’s Bib Gourmand selections are a treasure trove. These restaurants offer high-quality meals at reasonable prices, making them a popular choice for both locals and visitors. With 89 Bib Gourmand establishments, the variety is vast, showcasing a range of cuisines and dining styles that cater to all tastes.

Cuisine Styles You Will Find in Singapore
#

Singapore’s culinary landscape is a melting pot of flavors, influenced by its diverse cultural heritage. The top cuisines represented in the Michelin Guide include:

  • Street Food: With 151 Michelin-rated street food options, Singapore is famous for its hawker centers and local delights.
  • Sushi: Featuring 11 sushi restaurants, the city offers authentic Japanese dining experiences.
  • Cantonese: An equal number of 11 restaurants serve Cantonese cuisine, showcasing traditional dishes with a modern twist.
  • Innovative: With 10 innovative establishments, chefs in Singapore are pushing culinary boundaries and experimenting with flavors.
  • Singaporean: Celebrating local flavors, 9 restaurants focus on authentic Singaporean dishes.
  • Italian: Featuring 8 Italian restaurants, the city offers a taste of Italy with contemporary flair.
  • French: With 7 French establishments, diners can indulge in classic and modern French cuisine.
  • European Contemporary: Another 7 restaurants offer a contemporary take on European dishes.
  • French Contemporary: 6 establishments serve modern French cuisine with innovative approaches.
  • Peranakan: Representing the unique Straits Chinese culture, 6 restaurants focus on Peranakan cuisine.

Price Ranges and What to Expect
#

When dining at Michelin-rated restaurants in Singapore, expect a range of price points to accommodate different budgets. The restaurants span from affordable street food options priced at around $ to high-end dining experiences that can cost $$$$.

  • $: Street food and casual dining options, such as Hill Street Tai Hwa Pork Noodle and Candlenut.
  • $$: Mid-range restaurants offering quality dishes without the high price tag, like Summer Pavilion and Shisen Hanten.
  • $$$: Fine dining establishments, including Iggy’s and Jaan by Kirk Westaway, where you can expect a more refined experience.
  • $$$$: High-end dining experiences, such as Les Amis and Odette, where culinary artistry and luxury come together.

How to Book and Tips for Dining
#

Reservations are highly recommended, especially for popular Michelin-starred restaurants. Many establishments offer online booking options, while others may require a phone call. It’s advisable to book well in advance to secure your table, particularly for the three-star and two-star restaurants.

When dining at Michelin-rated establishments, consider the following tips:

  1. Dress Code: Many high-end restaurants have a dress code, so it’s best to check in advance.
  2. Tasting Menus: Consider opting for a tasting menu to experience a range of dishes and the chef’s signature creations.
  3. Pairing Options: Inquire about wine or beverage pairings to enhance your dining experience.
  4. Timing: Arrive on time for your reservation, as many restaurants have strict seating policies.

With its rich culinary landscape and diverse Michelin-rated options, Singapore is a haven for food lovers. Whether you’re indulging in street food or savoring a multi-course meal at a three-star restaurant, the city promises an unforgettable dining experience that reflects its vibrant culture and culinary innovation.
